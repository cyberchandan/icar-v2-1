Indian Council of Agricultural Research

Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare

TENDER DOCUMENT

For

Migration, Redesign, Development and Maintenance of Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) website (icar.org.in)

1. Introduction

The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR), an autonomous body under the Department of Agriculture and Education (DARE), Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Government of India wishes to redesign and develop a new website and migrate the contents from its old website. The new website is expected to follow GIGW 3.0 guidelines and the best practices including Digital Brand Identity Manual 3.0 (DBIM), ensuring full compliance with security standards and government requirements. The present document provides the technical requirements of the proposed website. The proposal is through open tender inviting from eligible, experienced, and qualified organizations to undertake the migration, redesign, development, and maintenance of the ICAR’s bilingual website. The ICAR also wishes to introduce a text-based AI-enabled chat bot help various type of stakeholders to navigate the website and get their queries resolved quickly. Another component included in this proposal is to develop a separate robust E-Commerce Platform to showcase and sell various types of products from constituent units of ICAR. It is also expected that the new website shall be cloud ready, deployable on a secured government cloud with necessary security audit certification for Government applications. The existing website is in Drupal 10 running in RHEL 9.5 with MYSQL 8.0 as the DBMS. The selected vendor will be responsible for the complete lifecycle of the website, including its development, migration from Drupal 10 to Drupal 11.3. (or latest stable version), deployment on a Government Cloud, as well as ensuring ongoing maintenance, security, and compliance for a period of initially for 1 year(after one year of warranty from Go Live date), and further extendable for 2 years+2 years based on certain conditions given in the financial bid.

2. Objectives

The primary objectives of this tender are:

  1. To redesign and develop the ICAR's website (bilingual - English & Hindi) to meet current technology standards and user experience requirements.
  2. To migrate the existing contents from Drupal 10 to Drupal 11.3.x (or latest stable version), ensuring all functionalities, content, security standards are properly transitioned and server administration.
  3. To ensure compliance with GIGW 3.0 and other relevant regulations including DBIM 3.0.
  4. To develop a text-based AI-Assisted Chat Bot Assistant to provide navigational and other help for the visitors as per Annexure A.
  5. To create a separate E-Commerce Module with the same look-and-feel of the ICAR website for online selling of ICAR commercial products as per Annexure B.
  6. To provide support and maintenance services for the website for a period of 1 year (after the website goes live) and further extendable for 2 years + 2 years as per the detailed T&C mentioned below. The services shall include regular security patching, compliance checks, performance monitoring, and troubleshooting work.
3. Scope of Work

The scope of work for the migration, redesign, development, and maintenance of the ICAR website includes, but is not limited to, the following activities:

i.  Provide Software Requirement Specification (SRS) Document
  • Develop and submit a comprehensive Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document for the project in alignment with the requirements of ICAR. This document will outline the functional, technical, and operational requirements for the website, ensuring a clear understanding between all stakeholders. The document shall also include a plan for maintaining Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) as per the guidelines of CERT-IN (available at https://www.cert-in.org.in/PDF/TechnicalGuidelines-on-SBOM,QBOM&CBOM,AIBOM_and_HBOM_ver2.0.pdf).
ii.  Migration and Development of Website from Drupal 10 to Drupal 11.3.x (or latest stable version)
  • Perform the migration of the existing website from Drupal 10 to Drupal 11.x (or the latest stable version of Drupal) as per the approved SRS. This will include updating or rewriting modules, themes, and develop custom functionalities to ensure full compatibility with the new version, while ensuring minimal disruption to services during the transition. The website shall be in two languages i.e. English and Hindi. The technical content and its Hindi translations would be provided by ICAR.
iii.  Theme Development
  • Design and develop a custom website theme that adheres to the guidelines prescribed by the Government of India including Digital Brand Identity Manual 3.0 (DBIM), ensuring the theme is responsive, accessible, and compliant with GIGW 3.0 (Guidelines for Indian Government Websites). The theme should include the flexibility to change colour schemes, allowing for future customization based on the client's requirements.
  • A minimum of three different themes to be provided to the department as options.
iv.  Basic Requirements
  • Submission Portal by authorized users for news item with necessary workflow for review/editing/publishing
  • AI enabled Virtual Assistant for allowing users to find relevant pages/information as per the details provided in Annexure A
  • E-Commerce module for listing of ICAR Commercial Products (Necessary option to list, search, shopping cart, shipping address, user registration etc.) as a separate application as per the requirements given in Annexure B
v.  Google Analytics Integration and Management
  • Integrate Google Analytics into the website to enable tracking of website performance, user behaviour, traffic sources, and other key metrics. Provide ongoing management and configuration of Google Analytics to ensure accurate data collection and reporting, tailored to the needs of ICAR.
  • Summarized monthly reports from Google Analytics through emails to selected users (or on the website itself). Monthly reports may be required for individual/group of pages (like SMD-wise, news items, circulars etc).
vi.  ICAR Social Media Integration
  • Integrate ICAR’s social media channels (such as Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, YouTube etc.) into the website. This may include embedding social media feeds, sharing buttons, or other functionalities that allow users to interact with the social media accounts directly from the website
vii.  Website Testing
  • Conduct comprehensive testing of the website across various browsers, devices, and screen sizes to ensure functionality, responsiveness, and performance. This includes functional testing, user acceptance testing (UAT), cross-browser compatibility testing, mobile compatibility testing, and performance testing to ensure the website meets all specified requirements. The testing results shall be made available to ICAR.
viii.  Deployment of Website and Database on Government Cloud
  • Deploy the fully functional website and database onto the Government Cloud Virtual Machines (VMs) hired by the ICAR. This process will involve the transfer of all files, databases, configurations, and customizations to the cloud environment, ensuring a smooth transition to the production environment.
ix.  Cloud VM Creation for Webserver and Database Server
  • Create two separate Virtual Machines (VMs) on the Government Cloud infrastructure: one for hosting the webserver and another for the database server. These VMs will be provisioned with sufficient resources (CPU, RAM, storage) to ensure optimal performance of the website.
x.  Cloud VM Configuration and Installation
  • Configure the VMs with
    • Necessary Operating system with latest security patches
    • Firewall configuration to protect the server from unauthorized access.
    • Webserver (e.g., Apache/Nginx) for serving website content.
    • Database Server (e.g., MySQL/MariaDB) for managing website data and securing from unauthorized access/blocking default users.
    • PHP, phpMyAdmin, and PHP-FPM for dynamic content generation and database management.
    • SSL Certificate (annual renewal) for secure, encrypted communication between users and the website. The configuration of SSL certificate is to enable https service for secured access
    • SMTP Relay for email services.
    • Secure FTP (SFTP) for secure file transfers.
    • Drupal 11.3.x or latest stable version, Composer, Drush, and all required Drupal modules for website functionality.
    • Log configuration for all services to ensure proper monitoring, error tracking, and performance auditing
xi.  Security Hardening of VMs/Servers
  • Implement security hardening measures on both the webserver and database server to protect the website and its infrastructure from security vulnerabilities. This includes disabling unnecessary services, applying security patches from time to time, configuring secure access methods (e.g., SSH keys), and enhancing the overall server security posture
xii.  Server Administration for Government of India Compliances
  • Provide ongoing server administration, ensuring compliance with all relevant Government of India standards and guidelines. This includes performing regular patching, updates, and necessary upgrades to the server operating system, Drupal 11.x and associated modules or the latest stable versions to ensure the infrastructure remains secure and compliant with Government of India IT policies.
xiii.  Annual Security Audit of Website
  • Conduct an annual security audit to assess the website’s vulnerabilities, risks, and overall security posture as a compulsory measure. This should be done on periodic basis as well whenever there will be any changes in the server configurations/code changes. This audit will evaluate the website’s compliance with best practices and identify any areas for improvement to ensure that the website remains “safe to host” in compliance with industry standards and Government of India guidelines.
xiv.  GIGW 3.0 Compliance and Certification from STQC
  • Ensure the website complies with the Guidelines for Indian Government Websites 3.0 (GIGW). The project team will prepare document and implement necessary changes to achieve GIGW 3.0 compliance. The website will be audited and certified by STQC (Standardization Testing and Quality Certification) to meet the requirements of government standards.

5. Responsibilities of the successful bidder

The successful bidder shall provide support through Support Team Engineers for one year from GoLive as Warranty Period and followed by one year as AMC, which can be extended to 2 + 2 years as per the terms and conditions mentioned in the financial bid, who will be responsible for ensuring the smooth operation, maintenance, and compliance of the ICAR website and associated infrastructure. These engineers will manage the ongoing tasks of website maintenance, security monitoring, technical support, content updates, server administration, and other essential services as outlined below.

The activities and responsibilities of the Support Team Engineers include but are not limited to the following:

  1. Website Maintenance, Security Monitoring, Patching, Updating, Security Auditing, and GIGW Auditing (Including Surveillance Auditing)
    • Website Maintenance: Provide continuous support for website upkeep, ensuring the website is available, functional, and optimized at all times. Regular updates will be applied to the content, structure, and modules of the website, in line with project requirements or as per ICAR’s instructions. Ensure that the broken links are identified both by automated scripts and by user feedbacks and corrected immediately.
    • Training: Provide periodic training to identified personnel on updating webpages, security practices
    • Performance Monitoring of AI Chatbot: Provide continuous performance monitoring and retraining of the AI powered Chatbot in case required and report the user satisfaction level
    • E-Commerce Site Maintenance: Maintain E-Commerce Site and secure from cyberattacks, provide support to ICAR team managing the E-Commerc
    • Security Monitoring: Proactively monitor the website and server infrastructure for security threats, vulnerabilities, or unauthorized activities. Use automated tools and manual monitoring processes to ensure the website’s security is up to date.
    • Patching & Updating: Apply necessary security patches and updates to both the Drupal platform, associated modules, and underlying server infrastructure. This includes critical software updates, bug fixes, and performance enhancements as they become available.
    • Security Auditing: Perform annual security audits to identify and mitigate potential risks, ensuring the site adheres to Government of India Guidelines and Compliance. Implement recommendations from security audits and continuously assess the security posture.
    • GIGW Auditing: Ensure the website remains compliant with the Guidelines for Indian Government Websites (GIGW) 3.0. Regular auditing will be conducted to maintain compliance, focusing on aspects such as accessibility, usability, and security. This includes Surveillance Auditing, as required by the Government of India, to track any vulnerabilities or deviations from the guidelines.
  2. ICAR Social Media Content Linking / Updating
    • Social Media Content Management: Ensuring that content related to ICAR’s social media accounts is properly integrated with the website. This includes linking or embedding social media feeds, updates, and posts on the website to ensure a seamless user experience
  3. Google Analytics Reporting
    • Google Analytics Monitoring: Oversee the integration and monitoring of Google Analytics on the website to track visitor traffic, behaviour patterns, and other key performance indicators (KPIs)
    • Reporting: Generate and provide periodic reports based on the insights derived from Google Analytics, such as user behavior, traffic sources, conversion rates, and website performance. These reports will be used for decision-making and optimization of the website’s content and design.
  4. Technical Issue Troubleshooting and Fixing
    • Issue Resolution: The support team will be the primary point of contact for troubleshooting and resolving any technical issues that may arise. This includes addressing functionality errors, performance issues, server downtime, or security breaches.
    • Root Cause Analysis: The support team will perform thorough investigations to identify the root causes of issues, ensuring that solutions are implemented in a timely and efficient manner to restore website functionality
  5. Server Administration
    • Server Monitoring and Management: The support team will overseeing the server infrastructure that hosts the website and database. This includes monitoring server health, ensuring system resources (CPU, memory, storage) are within optimal limits, and performing preventive maintenance
    • Configuration and Optimization: Perform server configurations and optimizations, including adjusting webserver settings (Apache/Nginx), database configurations, PHP, and other server-side applications, to ensure that the website runs smoothly and securely
    • Backup and Recovery: Regularly back up the website database, codebase, files and configurations, ensuring that backup processes are in place to protect against data loss. The engineers will be responsible for verifying the integrity of backup systems and executing disaster recovery procedures if necessary.
  6. Preparing Government Compliance Related Reports as and When Required
    • Compliance Reporting: The support team will assist ICAR in preparing reports related to government compliance as required by regulatory bodies. This includes compiling documentation and data related to security, GIGW, accessibility, and other compliance requirements for government websites.
    • Adherence to Guidelines: The support team will ensure that all website updates, content, and infrastructure changes meet relevant Government of India standards and guidelines and will prepare any necessary reports for submission to government authorities or auditors
  7. Communication with NIC Support and Managing Services through NIC’s eForms
    • Liaison with NIC Support: The support team will act as the point of contact with NIC (National Informatics Centre) for any support or technical assistance required regarding the website infrastructure, hosting services, or network configurations. This includes coordinating with NIC for resolving issues related to server uptime, connectivity, or software configurations.
    • Managing NIC Services via eForms: The support team will manage services and requests through NIC’s eForms platform, including tasks such as provisioning new services, troubleshooting issues, or making updates to cloud resources and infrastructure. They will ensure timely submission of forms and handling of service requests in alignment with NIC protocols.
    • Communication with NIC Support and Managing Services through NIC’s eForms: Integration of NIC eforms in the ICAR website and communication with cloud service providers
5.1 Overall Responsibilities and Skills:
  • Availability and Response Time: The support team must be available on call 24/7/365 and provide timely responses to issues and requests. They should follow established SLAs for response times, troubleshooting, and issue resolution. The support team shall always be in touch with the point of contacts from ICAR Side
  • Communication and Reporting: Clear and effective communication with ICAR, NIC/Government support, and other relevant stakeholders will be crucial. The support team should provide periodic progress updates, as well as detailed reports related to website performance, security, and compliance
  • Knowledge and Expertise: The support team should have strong technical skills, particularly in web technologies, server administration, and security practices. Familiarity with Drupal, Linux-based systems, and cloud infrastructure is essential for performing the required tasks effectively.
